Starting in 2024, I have been active in research with quantum computing, in the areas of variational quantum algorithms and approaches to simultaneous solving processes. The idea is that one can observe the solution process on one problem instance to get some idea of how to approach another problem instance, so I (and the others in the Kent-Case Quantum Group) try to do just that. My very first paper, which you can read here, was accepted and presented at at QCCC-24 in June 2024. This was joint work with Eric Yarnot, with help and contributions from several others whose names are in the PDF.

I started to get seriously involved with programming in late 2019, when I was a tenth-grader at Davis Senior High. Since then, I have developed projects in four main languages, in teams and alone, for a variety of applications. Languages:

  • Highly proficient in C
  • Proficient in Python
  • Proficient in Java
  • Intermediate in C++
  • Beginner in Scheme and JavaScript
  • Enough HTML/CSS to make this page

Besides C, which I have used a large amount, each of C++/Java/Python I have used roughly equally. The varying proficiency levels in them is mainly due the time since last major use, and for C++ particularly, knowledge (or lack thereof) of "idiomatic", "modern" styles.

While the full extent of my projects and tools is available on my GitHub page (both my own repositories and the organizations I am part of), here is a rundown of some of the highlights:

  • cml (Summer 2023): from-scratch machine-learning framework in C.
  • Y-flat (2022): incomplete compiler (with relating tooling done by Jake Roggenbuck among others) for my custom programming language Y-flat. Several thousand lines of C code, with a lexer, parser, analyzer, etc. all custom-made.
  • Minecraft clone (2021): a sandbox world generator, modeled and textured after Minecraft, that I made using OpenGL and C++.
  • QME (2021): an (incomplete) 4X strategy game whose development I led/co-led, collaboratively with several programmers, artists, and designers. This was a wonderful experience in collaborative development and teamwork.
